Intraday trading is high risk. This engine is for controlled paper/assisted testing first. Live broker execution should only be enabled after verified paper performance, strict loss limits, and compliant broker/API setup.
Research Lab checks the strategy on old market days before you trust it on new days. It does not show tomorrow’s picks. It shows what worked, what failed, and what the system should avoid or boost in future scans.
Backtest = past simulation only. It checks old market days, records what would have worked, and builds lessons for future scans. It does not show tomorrow’s stock list.
How much history is enough? More trades per pattern gives a more trustworthy win rate (roughly ±18% margin of error at 8 trades vs ±5% at 100 trades), but calendar length matters separately: a strategy also needs to be tested across a real bull run, a choppy/sideways stretch, and ideally a real correction/bear phase, not just one market mood. 3 years is a reasonable starting minimum; 5-10 years gives a better chance of covering multiple regimes, though very old data for some symbols may be thinner or less reliable.
Data correctness for splits/bonus issues: Backtests fetch each stock's corporate-action history alongside prices. A confirmed stock split or bonus issue (common on NSE/BSE) is detected and excluded both from the data-integrity check and from any simulated trade whose holding window would straddle it, so a real split cannot be mistaken for a fake crash or a fake stop-loss hit.
Raw vs Learned: Raw replays the base strategy exactly as coded, with no pattern-memory or per-stock history applied — this is the honest control/baseline. Learned re-scores and filters the same test days using every lesson the system has recorded so far (pattern penalties, regime weighting, per-stock strength). Use "Compare Raw vs Learned" to run both on the identical window and see whether learning actually improved the result, and by how much. There is no target of a perfect win rate here — a small, consistent, positive edge across many trades (profit factor above 1.0, held up across regimes) is the realistic goal.

How stock selection works: “Quality stocks to scan” means the starting tradable universe. The app first removes inactive, illiquid, old-symbol, low-volume, low-turnover, or bad-history stocks. Then, on each old test date, it ranks the remaining quality stocks by bullish setup, volume confirmation, risk/reward, market type, past pattern behavior, and stock-specific history. It is not random.
Data integrity: New backtests record whether prices are raw or adjusted, the market-data source, and the candle-quality checks used before learning. Suspicious candle data is blocked so wrong prices do not become wrong learning.
